The project is a simulation study of a thermally drifting thermo-optical system in which a Gaussian beam is phase-modulated by an SLM, propagates through a medium whose optical response changes over time because of heat-driven refractive-index variation, and produces an output intensity pattern at a detector. The thermal evolution of the medium is modeled with the heat equation and linked to optics through the thermo-optic effect, while a physics-informed neural network is used as a surrogate for the temperature field so that the resulting drift in the effective optical operator can be tracked. The overall control objective is then to choose SLM phase patterns that maintain or recover high intensity at a desired target location despite this thermal drift
